Louis Pasteur was born on December 27, 1822, in Dole, France. He was a French microbiologist and chemist who discovered that microorganisms cause disease. He developed the process of pasteurization, which involves heating liquids to kill harmful bacteria.

He spent most of his life studying bacteria and ways to prevent many illnesses.

He also invented pasteurization which heats milk to kill harmful bacteria.

He also developed vaccines for rabies and anthrax.

Louis Pasteur died on September 29, 1895, in Marnes-la-Coquette, France, at the age of 72.

His famous quote is "Chance favors the prepared mind."
